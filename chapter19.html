Chapter 19: Practical Information

This chapter covers SEO Optimization, analytics, and publishing your site.

Search Engine optimization (SEO)

SEO is the practice of getting your website to show up closer to the top when people search for related topics. Search engines not only look at what appears on your site, but also how many sites link to you (and how relevant they are.) SEO is often split into two areas: on page techniques and off page techniques.

Off Page Techniques

Off page techniques are pretty small in how much you can do to help. Really you need other pages to link to you. Search engines really look at the words between the <a> tags. It may be considered more relevant if it contains more keywords.

On Page Techniques

On page techniques are the most effective, starting with keywords. You want to have kjeywords that people will enter into a search engine, and include these words in your HTML and text for your site. There are 7 places you want your site to have keywords:

1. Page Title
The page title. Sits inside the title element, inside the head element.
2. URL/Web Address
Name of file is part of web address. Where possible, use keywords in file name.
3. Headings
If a keyword is in a heading (h1, h2, h3, etc) then the search engine will know the page is about that subnject. Gives this text greater weight.
4. Text
Helps to repeat the keyword 2-3 times in the main body of the text (where possible.) You don't want to overuse the word.
5. Link Text
Use keywords in the text that creates links between pages (instead of phrases like "click here.")
6. Image Alt Text
Accurate alt text for images will help them show up in image searches.
7. Page Descriptions
Also lives inside the head element, specified using a meta tag. It should be a sentence that describes the content of the page. These sentences won't be shown on the page, but might show up in results page on a search engine.
There are 6 steps to identifying keywords:

1. Brainstorm: Think of keywords that describe your content.
2. Organize: Group keywords in separate lists for separate sections of your site.
3. Research: Use websites like google to find similar keywords. You can also use Wordtracker and Keyworddiscovery.
4. Compare: Use tools like googles advanced search to see how much competition you have for your keywords.
5. Refine: Pick the most relevant keywords to focus on. You should stiff use phrases, even if there is a lot of competition.
6. Map: Pick 3-5 keywords or phrases that map to each page of your website, and use them as keywords for each page. You don't need to repeat the same keywords on all the pages. As you get further away from the home page, your keywords will likely become more specific.

Analytics

The first thing you should do is sign up for the Google Analytics Service. It will give you a piece of code to add on every page of your site. Analytics gives you a wealth of info, as well as tools like:

  • the number of visits
  • the number of unique visits
  • total number of page views
  • avg number of pages a visitor looks at
  • avg time on site
  • a date selector to check within a range
  • export this information for excel
  • what users are looking at
  • where they came from.

Domain Names and Hosting

Usually, you have to pay an annual fee to keep a domain name once you find one. As for web hosting, there are a few things you should look out for. The amount of disk space offered, the bandwith, backups, if they offer email accounts, server-side languages and databases.
Many services will require you to use ftp to upload files to the server, as well as a username and password. You will probably need a ftp application, some common ones are:
FileZilla, FireFTP, CuteFTP, and SmartFTP.
Here are some services commonly created by web developers:
Blogs: Wordpress, Tumblr, Posterous.
E-Commerce: Shopify, Big Cartel, Magento.
Email Newsletter: Campaign Monitor and Mail Chimp.
Social Network Sharing Buttons: Add This and Add to Any.
