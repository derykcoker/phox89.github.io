Chapter 18: Process and Design

This chapter talks about the process for getting your demographic and planning the layout for your website.

Demographics

Seriously, a lot of the information here is just common sense. Think about who your website is for, and cater to them. English speaking country? Write your page in English. For kids? Keep information a little more simple. Selling something? Think about what you're selling and who wil be buying it.

Design

The first thing you'll want to do is to create a site map. This will help you plan how you want all your pages to sync up with each other. A good method for sorting out where information should go is card sorting. This will help to make sure all your information is organised, and nothing gets repeated.
Next, it's a good idea to create a wireframe. A wireframe should almost always be used, especially for planning. It would be useful to show to client before building an entire page, to see if the client likes the layout. Some popular ways to create wireframes are the 960.gs system, Go Mocking Bird, and Lovely Charts.

Visual Hierarchy

Even though this again is common sense, remember to group together related content into blocks or chunks. Once this is accomplished, each block can have its own small edits to make the important information stand out. Text size, color, and/or font style can all have a simple but large impact.
Page 469-472 have some fantastic example images that showcase how you can improve your webpage design. Headings to show what a block is about, clear and concise navigation, navigation that is interactive, contextual, and consistent.
